Airbnb is a startup founded in 2008 in San Francisco and expand rapidly. Nowadays, Airbnb serves more than 190 countries across the world. And it became one of the popular choices among the backpackers as an alternative choice besides normally hotel booking which has quite high cost. Renters could reserve a rental from both on a smartphone and website.

If a landlord would like to rent out the rentals with Airbnb or even other platforms, by knowing the appropriate rental rates is important, so landlord could set the rental rate that makes profit while not too high and no renter would rent.

This project has utilised Big Data from Airbnb to developed Linear Regression and Support Vector Regression (SVR) models to predict rental rates. Furthermore, this project has also done the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and gained the insights.

Landlords could be benefited from this project both from insights and rental rates prediction models to aid the decision, for instance, to add a bathroom, add beds, add accommodations, or even considering a neighbourhood if they would like to purchase a new real estate with respect to rental rate potential.

# Executive Summary
---

1. This project has developed rental rates prediction models to be an alternative decision aider for landlords to set the rental rates that make profit while not too high and no renter would rent.
2. The models have various adjustable factors including neighbourhood, room type, accommodations, beds, and bathrooms to predict a result as a rental rate.
3. This project has developed Regression Models which are Linear Regression and Support Vector Regression (SVR) to predict rental rates from Big Data. The most accurate model is Linear Regression which has Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) equals 46 USD and R-squared equals 0.52, for more information please refer to [Rental Rates Prediction Models](#bullet2). 
4. This project has done the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and gained the following insights in brief:
    - Manhattan and Brooklyn have the highest median of rental rates, which are 129 and 92 USD respectively.
    - Hotel room and entire home/apt room types have the highest median of rental rates, which are 201 and 149 USD respectively.
    - If landlords would like to set a high rental rate, they could add beds or add accommodations.
    - Both minimum and maximum nights don’t affect the rental rates.
    - Most rooms have accommodations 1-2 persons (65% of all rooms), have 1-2 beds (90% of all rooms), and 1 bathroom (87% of all rooms).

# Exploratory Data Analysis to obtain Insights
---
This project has done the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and gained the insights which can discuss in the following:

### 1) Rental Rates for Different Neighbourhoods
![https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/1.png](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/1.png)

1. Manhattan and Brooklyn have the highest variance of rental rates. (Some rooms have very high rental rates; some rooms have very low rental rates.)
2. Bronx, Brooklyn, Manhattan, Queens, and Staten Island neighbourhoods have the median of rental rates equal 72, 92, 129, 70, and 86 USD respectively.

If landlords already have their rentals, they could understand the median of rental rates for this neighbourhood. Or if examine at the size of a box plot which is 50% of all rooms, they could understand the range of rental rate for rentals in this neighbourhood.

But if landlords would like to purchase real estates from potential areas to rent them out in the future, they might consider Manhattan and Brooklyn since these neighbourhoods are likely to have high rental rates and be able to make profits.


### 2) Rental Rates for Different Room Types

![https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/2.png](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/2.png)

1. Entire home/apt and hotel room types have the high variance of rental rates. (Some rooms have very high rental rates; some rooms have very low rental rates.)
2. Entire home/apt, hotel room, private room, and shared room types have the median of rental rates equal 149, 201, 64, and 48 USD respectively.

This information informs that besides hotel rooms, renting out entire home/apt is likely to have higher rental rates than renting out private or shared rooms.

### 3) The Number of Room Types for Different Neighbourhoods

![https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/3.png](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/3.png)

1. Manhattan has more entire home/apt rooms than private rooms, 76%.
2. Queens has more private rooms than entire home/apt rooms, 67%.
3. Brooklyn, Staten Island, and Bronx have a similar number of entire home/apt rooms and private rooms.

### 6) Rental Rates versus Accommodations

1. 91% of rooms have accommodations between 1-4 persons.

![https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/6.png](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/6.png)

Knowing that how many accommodations most rooms have, landlords would understand that accommodations between 1-2 persons is not too low, since most rooms (approximately 66%) would set the accommodations around these values.

2. The higher accommodations, the higher likelihood of higher rental rate.

![https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/7.png](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/7.png)

If landlords would like to set a high rental rate, they could renovate to increase accommodations.

### 7) Rental Rates versus Beds

1. 99% of rooms have beds between 1-4 beds.

![https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/8.png](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/8.png)

2. The higher beds, the higher likelihood of higher rental rate.

![https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/9.png](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/9.png)

If landlords would like to set a high rental rate, they could add more beds. Adding more beds can increase accommodations (from the correlation analysis found that these 2 factors have a high correlation) which leads to be able to increase rental rate.

### 8) Rental Rates versus Bathrooms

1. 87% of rooms have 1 bathroom.

![https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/10.png](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/10.png)

2. The number of bathrooms has no correlation with rental rate.

![https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/11.png](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/11.png)

Although the high rental rate rooms would have more bathrooms, but that didn’t increase the rental rates. The high number of bathrooms could be a result from the high accommodations. And the rooms they have high accommodations are likely to have high rental rates.  Although, adding a bathroom would not increase a rental rate, but it would enhance renters’ experience. If the rental has positive reviews, they would attract the next potential renters.

### 9) Maximum Nights

![https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/12.png](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/12.png)

1. Maximum nights must also agree with the local laws in each country, which is how many maximum nights limit for a short stay.
2. 48% of rooms had set maximum nights at 3 years.
3. 12% of rooms had set maximum nights at 1 year.
4. 7% of rooms had set maximum nights at 30 days.


### 10) Minimum Nights

![https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/13.png](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ProStepSky/ProStepSky.github.io/master/img/1_Airbnb/13.png)

1. The most popular minimum nights is 30 days. The second most popular minimum nights and so on are few days.
2. 53% of rooms had set minimum nights at 30 days.
3. 14% of rooms had set minimum nights at 1 day.
4. 11% of rooms had set minimum nights at 2 days.

Both minimum and maximum nights have no correlation with any factors at all. Therefore, if the landlords would like to rent out the rental, they could set the number of minimum and maximum nights that suits their needs without worrying if the number of nights would affect the rental rate. The only one thing to be concerned is the maximum nights that must agree with the local laws in each country, which is how many maximum nights limit for a short stay.

# Conclusions

In conclusion, landlords could be benefited from this project both from insights and rental rates prediction models that have various adjustable factors. For instance, bathrooms, beds, etc. Which could aid the decision if the landlords would like to add a bathroom, add beds, add accommodations, or even considering a neighbourhood if they would like to purchase a new real estate with respect to rental rate potential.

# Future Scopes
---

1. This is an imbalanced dataset due to the imbalanced room types and imbalanced neighbourhoods, as shown in the proportion of room types pie chart and proportion of neighbourhoods pie chart in the Exploratory Data Analysis. The imbalanced dataset problem is pretty common in the data collection. The solution is to collect the following data more:
    - Shared rooms and hotel rooms.
    - Rooms from Queens, Bronx, and Staten Island.
    
    This could mitigate the imbalanced dataset problem and leads to more models’ accuracies.

2. Consider using the latitude and longitude values in addition. The assumption is the rooms that easy to travel, for instance, near the airport, might have higher rental rates. This way might lead to more accuracy of the models.
3. This project used the quantity data to make a prediction which utilised Logistic Regression and Support Vector Regression models. Since this dataset has the high fluctuated and imbalanced data, in the future, consider using Deep Learning techniques to assess whether they improve the accuracy.
